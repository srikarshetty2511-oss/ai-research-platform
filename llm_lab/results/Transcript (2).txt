### **DCU/ECTS Credits**

*   A student attending full-time at the University will normally accumulate 60 ECTS (European Credit Transfer System) credits per year. A 3-year degree programme would have a total ECTS credit value of 180 and, similarly, a 4-year degree programme would have a value of 240 ECTS credits.

**Assessment Methodology**

*   In general, student performance is determined at the conclusion of each academic year. In taught programmes, this is based on a combination of attainment in end-of-semester examinations (both semesters) and continuous assessment of academic exercises carried out throughout the academic year. Research programmes of study are assessed on the basis of a submitted thesis and oral examination; although in the case of Masters Degrees the latter may be waived at the discretion of the examiners.

**Pass Mark**

*   The minimum mark required for a pass in a module is 40% unless stated otherwise in programme regulations. A candidate who fails to attain the required pass standard in all modules taken at a single sitting may, at the discretion of the Progression and Award Boards, be allowed to pass the module as a whole by compensation, subject to the approval of Academic Council.

**Exemptions**

*   In general, all modules passed by a candidate in a particular year of study should have such passes held in perpetuity unless removed as a consequence of a subsequent disciplinary decision approved by Academic Council.
